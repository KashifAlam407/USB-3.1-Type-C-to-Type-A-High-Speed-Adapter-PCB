HIGH SPEED DESIGN REPORT

Project:
USB Type-C to USB Type-A Adapter (USB 3.0)

1. Differential Pair Routing

* USB 3.0 SuperSpeed TX/RX signals are routed as differential pairs.
* Differential pairs should be routed with matched lengths.
* Maintain constant trace width and spacing.
* Avoid sharp 90° bends; use 45° bends.

2. Signal Integrity Considerations

* Keep SuperSpeed traces as short as possible.
* Minimize vias on high-speed signals.
* Avoid routing high-speed traces across split ground planes.
* Maintain continuous ground reference beneath differential pairs.

3. USB Type-C Orientation Handling

* TUSB320 detects cable orientation using CC1 and CC2 pins.
* HD3SS6126 USB 3.0 multiplexer routes SuperSpeed lanes according to orientation.
* SEL signal from TUSB320 controls the HD3SS6126 multiplexer.

4. ESD Protection

* TPD4E05U06DQA used for USB 2.0 signal protection.
* PRTR5V0U2X devices used for USB 3.0 differential pair protection.
* ESD devices placed close to connector interfaces.

5. Power Integrity

* USB VBUS protected using TVS diode and resettable fuse.
* AP2112K-3.3 regulator generates 3.3 V supply.
* Decoupling capacitors placed near IC power pins.

6. PCB Layout Recommendations

* Use a solid GND plane.
* Place HD3SS6126 close to the USB Type-C connector.
* Keep differential pair lengths matched.
* Keep ESD components close to connectors.
* Place decoupling capacitors adjacent to IC power pins.

7. Conclusion
   The design follows standard USB 3.0 high-speed routing practices including differential pair routing, orientation detection, signal protection, power regulation, and impedance-aware PCB layout recommendations.
